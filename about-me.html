About Me

The doctor of the future is you

That’s not just a catchy phrase — it’s the truth I’ve lived, and the truth I now teach every person I work with.

My Roots in Healing

I was born in South Korea and lived with my grandparents until age nine. They modeled everyday wellness—home-cooked meals, garden herbs and bitter teas, daily walking in all weather, and weekly hot/cold therapy at the communal bathhouse.

My aunt was a pharmacist who also practiced Traditional Chinese Medicine, so I grew up around herbal formulas, acupuncture, and “meridian” checks. Those experiences taught me early that healing can take many forms.

Today, I see it as my first lesson: healing comes in many forms.

Moving, Studying and Initial Stress

After moving to the United States, life became about stress and achievement. Suddenly, wellness was replaced with stress—trying to fit in, trying to succeed. By college, I was surviving on coffee, Diet Coke, and ambition. Pharmacy school demanded everything, and though I looked successful on the outside, anxiety, exhaustion, and sleepless nights were quietly eroding my health.

The Irony of Being a “Health Expert”

After successfully completing my studies, I continued to develop my skill:

  • By 2006, I became a licensed pharmacist
  • By 2008, I had fellowship training in functional medicine
  • By 2012, I opened my own wellness pharmacy

People came to me for answers and natural solutions. Meanwhile, I was completely falling apart. Severe heartburn kept me awake every night. I had breakouts like a teenager. Nothing—no supplement, no diet—could touch my constant fatigue.

The worst part? I was having reactions to the very supplements I told my patients to take. My body was shutting down, and I kept ignoring all the warning signs.

Health Crisis

In June 2015, at age 33, I had a heart attack (NSTEMI) and received a stent. Despite exercising and eating “clean,” I missed the bigger picture. Three weeks into recovery, an integrative physician colleague suspected deeper issues. Testing revealed:

  • Bartonella
  • Mold toxicity
  • Mycoplasma pneumoniae
  • Epstein–Barr virus
  • Parasites
  • Heavy metals

Recovery was difficult. I experienced intense Herxheimer reactions (die off) and significant financial strain. In that season, I believe God met me and helped me connect key patterns: mitochondrial dysfunction, microbiome imbalance, environmental toxicity, and nervous system dysregulation. My body wasn’t failing—it was crying for help. Improvement came from restoring balance, not adding more pills. It was neither quick nor easy—but it worked.

Who I Help

If this describes you, I understand completely. I've been there. My job is to listen, connect the pieces that others miss, and help your body remember how to heal itself.
